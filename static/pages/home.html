About Peter Betlem

Hello!

My name is Peter, and I recently graduated with a PhD degree from the University Centre of Svalbard (UNIS) and the University of Oslo (UiO). The PhD thesis and more information is available at the UiO:DUO Research Archive.

I am also one of the co-founders behind GeoMonkey AS, a geospatial-oriented startup working with unmanned aerial vehicles, geospatial data bases/-portals/-sets, and quantitative videography. Feel free to send me a message if you want to know more.

I apply numbers and programmatic solutions to geoscientific challenges, whether through numerical models, structure-from-motion photogrammetry, GIS or otherwise.

My PhD targeted the imaging of fluid migration pathways in cap rock and overburden. The project builds upon the Longyearbyen CO2 Lab, a pilot CO2 sequestration project in Longyearbyen, Svalbard and contributes to the Norwegian CCS Research Centre. A large component of the work deals with the quantitative characterisation of 3D digital outcrop models in Svalbard and elsewhere, and I am one of the co-creators and current digital data lead of the Svalbox project.

Prior to starting my PhD in Longyearbyen I completed a MSc in Sustainable Energy at the University of Reykjavik, and before that an MSc in Molecular Chemistry at Radboud University, The Netherlands.

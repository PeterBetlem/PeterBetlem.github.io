About Peter Betlem

Hello!

My name is Peter, and I am currently a PhD Candidate working at the University Centre of Svalbard (UNIS).

I apply numbers and programmatic solutions to geoscientific challenges, whether through numerical models, structure-from-motion photogrammetry, GIS or otherwise.

My PhD targets the imaging of fluid migration pathways in caprock and overburden. The project builds upon the Longyearbyen CO2 Lab, a pilot CO2 sequestration project in Longyearbyen, Svalbard and contributes to the Norwegian CCS Research Centre. A large component of the work deals with the quantitative characterisation of 3D digital outcrop models in Svalbard and elsewhere, and I am one of the co-creators of the Svalbox project.

Prior to starting my PhD in Longyearbyen I completed a MSc in Sustainable Energy at the University of Reykjavik, and before that an MSc in Molecular Chemistry at Radboud University, The Netherlands.
